## 🎯 Project Objective

**What Drives Student Success?**

This project analyzes academic performance data of **10,000 students** across 17 variables to answer:

- How does the distribution of Final Grades look — High, Average, and At-Risk split?
- Does Exam Score vary by Stress Level and Gender?
- What is the relationship between Engagement Score and Exam Score?
- Which specific factors separate High Performers (A/B grades) from At-Risk students (D grades)?

## 📈 Key KPIs & Insights

| KPI | Value |
|-----|-------|
| 🎓 Total Students | 10,000 |
| 📊 Average Exam Score | 70.19% |
| 🏆 High Performers | 51.33% |
| ⚠️ At-Risk Students | 24.27% |
| 📉 Average Performers | 24.40% |

### 🔍 Key Findings

1. **Stress & Performance:** High-stress students scored ~1.3 points lower on average than low-stress peers, highlighting stress as a measurable risk factor.

2. **Gender Insights:** The 'Other' gender group outperformed with a 70.43% average exam score vs 70.20% (Female) and 70.16% (Male).

3. **Surprise Finding — Study Hours ≠ Success:** High performers study **27.19 hrs/week** vs At-Risk students **27.0 hrs/week** — almost identical! This shows study hours alone don't predict success; engagement, attendance, and stress management matter more.

4. **Engagement vs Score:** Higher engagement scores show a positive but non-linear correlation with exam performance.

5. **Grade Distribution:** A: 26.78% | B: 24.55% | C: 24.40% | D (At-Risk): 24.27%

## 🛠️ Dashboard Features

### 📋 Sheet Structure
- `Student Performance Dataset` — Raw cleaned data
- `grade distribution stress-exam` — Grade split + Stress vs Score analysis
- `High vs Low Performers` — Comparative analysis across behavioral factors
- `Engagement vs Score chart` — Scatter/line visualization
- `kpis sheets` — KPI calculation workbook
- `Sheet4` — Working analysis sheet
- `dashboard` — Final interactive dashboard

### ✅ Dashboard Components Built
- **KPI Summary Cards** with Conditional Formatting as visual alerts (Red = At-Risk threshold, Green = High Performance benchmark)
- **Interactive Slicers** for Gender, Learning Style, and Stress Level — stakeholder can filter without technical skills
- ** Chart Types:** Grade distribution column chart , Stress vs Exam scatter, High vs At-Risk comparison bars, Engagement vs Score scatter chart
- **Professional Header** with cohesive visual narrative layout
